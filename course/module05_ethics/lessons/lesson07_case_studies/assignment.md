# Assignment: Comprehensive Ethical Audit

## Objectives

- Conduct a full ethical audit of a real AI system
- Apply all concepts from Module 5
- Produce a professional audit report
- Develop actionable recommendations

## Instructions

Select a real, deployed AI system with publicly available information about its design and impact. You may not use a system analyzed in class. Some suggestions:

- **Apple Card** — credit limit algorithm (gender bias allegations)
- **Amazon Recruiting Tool** — resume screening (bias against women)
- **PredPol** — predictive policing
- **Upstart** — AI-powered lending platform
- **IBM Watson for Oncology** — cancer treatment recommendations
- **Clearview AI** — facial recognition
- **Zillow Offers** — home valuation algorithm
- **NHS COVID-19 App** — contact tracing

Conduct a full ethical audit following this framework. Your report should be 3000–4000 words.

### Report Structure

#### Executive Summary (300 words)
Briefly describe the system, key findings, and top recommendations.

#### Section 1: System Description (400 words)
- What does the system do?
- Who developed and deployed it?
- What data does it use?
- How widely is it deployed?
- What is the decision context (high-stakes? automated? human-in-the-loop?)

#### Section 2: Five Principles Analysis (600 words)
Analyze the system using beneficence, non-maleficence, autonomy, justice, and explicability. For each principle, provide evidence and reasoning.

#### Section 3: Bias and Fairness (500 words)
- What types of bias might be present?
- What does the evidence show about group-level disparities?
- Which fairness definitions are most relevant?
- Compute or estimate fairness metrics if possible.

#### Section 4: Transparency and Explainability (400 words)
- Can stakeholders understand how the system works?
- Are explanations provided to affected individuals?
- What explainability methods would be appropriate?

#### Section 5: Privacy (400 words)
- What personal data is collected?
- What are the privacy risks?
- Are privacy protections adequate?
- What would differential privacy require?

#### Section 6: Social Impact (400 words)
- Employment effects
- Distributional effects (who benefits, who is harmed?)
- Environmental impact (if applicable)
- Broader societal implications

#### Section 7: Regulatory Compliance (400 words)
- Which regulations apply?
- Under EU AI Act, what risk level?
- What compliance gaps exist?

#### Section 8: Recommendations (200 words)
Three specific, actionable recommendations with timelines.

### References
Use APA 7 format.

## Deliverables

A single PDF document (3000–4000 words).

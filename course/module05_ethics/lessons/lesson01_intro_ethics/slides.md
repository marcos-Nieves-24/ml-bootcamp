# Slides: Introduction to AI Ethics

## Slide 1: Title Slide
- Module 5: Ethics in Artificial Intelligence
- Lesson 1: Introduction to AI Ethics
- Course: Machine Learning for Data Analysis and SaaS

## Slide 2: Learning Objectives
- Define AI ethics
- Explain why it matters
- Identify five key principles
- Analyze real-world cases
- Evaluate ethical trade-offs

## Slide 3: Motivation — The Healthcare Algorithm
- 2019: Algorithm systematically underdiagnosed Black patients
- 94% accuracy overall, but failed for specific groups
- Accuracy is not enough

## Slide 4: What is AI Ethics?
- Moral principles applied to AI systems
- Questions of right and wrong in design, development, deployment
- Who is responsible? Who is harmed? Who decides?

## Slide 5: Why It Matters
- Harm prevention
- Trust
- Legal compliance (EU AI Act, GDPR)
- Better science

## Slide 6: Principle 1 — Beneficence
- Do good
- Maximize positive outcomes
- Serve the public good
- Example: diagnostic AI that improves patient outcomes

## Slide 7: Principle 2 — Non-maleficence
- Do no harm
- Direct harm (misdiagnosis)
- Indirect harm (bias, privacy)
- Long-term harm (environmental, social)

## Slide 8: Principle 3 — Autonomy
- Respect human agency
- Informed consent
- Right to opt out
- Human oversight for high-stakes decisions

## Slide 9: Principle 4 — Justice
- Be fair
- Distribute benefits equitably
- Protect vulnerable groups
- Access to redress

## Slide 10: Principle 5 — Explicability
- Be transparent and accountable
- Explain decisions
- Assign responsibility
- Example: credit score explanation

## Slide 11: Ethical Trade-offs
- Autonomy vs. Beneficence
- Privacy vs. Transparency
- Justice vs. Efficiency
- No universal formula

## Slide 12: Case: Predictive Policing
- Uses historical crime data
- Risks: perpetuates biased policing
- Five-principle analysis table

## Slide 13: Biotech Example — Drug Discovery
- Training data from European-derived cell lines
- Drug may be less effective for non-Europeans
- Dataset composition is an ethical decision

## Slide 14: SaaS Example — Loan Underwriting
- Zip code as feature → redlining by proxy
- Denied applicants deserve explanations
- Features that are predictive may not be ethical

## Slide 15: Common Mistakes
- Accuracy = ethics (false)
- Ignoring data provenance
- Ethics as a checkbox
- Assuming neutrality
- Forgetting the human

## Slide 16: Best Practices
- Start early
- Diverse teams
- Stakeholder engagement
- Document decisions
- Assume responsibility

## Slide 17: Summary
- Five principles: beneficence, non-maleficence, autonomy, justice, explicability
- Ethics is an engineering responsibility
- Next lesson: Bias and Fairness

## Slide 18: Key Terms
- Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Autonomy, Justice, Explicability
- AI ethics, Ethical trade-off
